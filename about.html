About me

I am a final year PhD student on the Systems Biology DTC at the University of Oxford . I work in an interdisciplinary field applying mathematical and computational techniques to biology. I develop and use Bayesian inference in cell and developmental biology.

I am based at the Wolfson Centre for Mathematical Biology in the Mathematical Institute and also collaborate with the Davis Lab in the Department of Biochemistry.

My research focuses on modelling of mRNA localisation in Drosophila development and model inference, particularly for models with likelihoods that are hard to compute. I am also interested in computational statistics and machine learning.

Education
